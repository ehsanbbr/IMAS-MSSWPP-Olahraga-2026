⏱️ Reporting Time Rules (Lapor Diri)

Per the official meet regulations, athletes must report to the call room before their event — not straight to the officials on the track/field. Minimum reporting time before the event starts:

Track races (Balapan)20 min before
Track — hurdles (Lari Berpagar)25 min before
Shot Put, Long Jump, Triple Jump30 min before
High Jump55 min before
Pole Vault70 min before
  • Athletes who report late may have their entry cancelled and be treated as having withdrawn.
  • Athletes may not report directly to technical officials in the field of play — it must go through the call room.
  • Call-room announcements for reporting are only made 3 times — please don't rely on hearing an announcement, watch the clock instead.
  • Athletes must wear their bib number pinned at all 4 corners and show it when reporting.
  • Reporting 15 minutes or later than the event start time means the athlete will not be allowed to compete at all.

🚫 Official Parking Notice

Important — from the organizer, via the coach: Parents, teachers, and students are not allowed to park or walk inside the school compound, including the canteen area.

📋 Rules Parents Should Know

A few key rules from the official meet regulations that could affect your child on the day:

  • Baton exchange zone (4×100m Relay) — the baton must be passed within the marked takeover zone. If it changes hands outside that zone, the team can be disqualified regardless of finishing position.
  • Missing an event = withdrawn from the rest — if an athlete skips a confirmed event without a medical officer's note, they're treated as having withdrawn from all their remaining registered events too, not just that one.
  • Arena access is restricted — only competing athletes and officials are allowed inside the competition arena itself; parents and spectators watch from outside it.
  • Warm-up area only — athletes may only warm up in the designated warm-up area, not anywhere on the field.
  • Medals — individual events award 1st–3rd place (1st–5th for the 3000m Open); relay teams award 6 medals split among the top 3 teams.
  • Spikes limit — max 9mm spike length on the track (12mm allowed for High Jump and Javelin only).

🚗 Parking & Getting There

The organizer has designated two official parking spots outside the school. From either one, you'll walk a short distance to the track, entering through a small gate that will be opened specially for spectators during the event.

  • Option A — Putrajaya Lake Club (Kelab Tasik Putrajaya) — closest option, along the lakeside. Walk west along Jalan P8 towards the track, roughly 5–7 minutes on foot.
  • Option B — Puspanita Courts (volleyball/tennis courts near Jalan P10a2/1) — walk south along Jalan P8, past the open field, roughly 8–10 minutes to the track.
  • Look out for event-day signage or marshals directing you to the spectator gate — its exact spot may vary slightly by day.
  • Both are a genuine walk in Putrajaya's heat — consider a hat, water, and comfortable shoes, especially for the Puspanita option.

This guidance is directly from the meet organizer (relayed by the coach) — please follow it rather than parking in the school compound.
